Success! Now, what is this?

To put it simply, this system is a proposed alternative to passwords called Dynauth (dynamic authentication).

  1. Users configure a table of "keys" associated with "locks"

    2. Locks -> Keys

    1. -> {{ randomWord }}
  2. On login, 4 of the locks are chosen at random and presented

    3. Locks:

    {{ lockString }}
  3. The user enters in the keys associated with the locks

    4. Keys:

    {{ key }}

That's it! Not so hard really.

There are some restrictions though:

  • Each key has to be at least 3 characters
  • None of the keys can repeat
  • During login, every time authentication fails, the locks are re-shuffled
  • The keys must be entered in without any delimiters when logging in
  • Please do not write them down anywhere!

Memorization techniques

Memorizing words in this fashion can seem difficult, but there are some simple tricks that make it much easier. Try following the instructions below when you are configuring your locks and keys.

  1. Pick 3 or 4 random words from this list

    2. Random words

    {{ randomWord }}
  2. Create a nonsensical sentence incorporating those words

    3. Random words given

    • Shark
    • Horn
    • Angel
    • Color

    Sentence constructed

    The 1winged 2angels are 3riding the 4silly 5horned 6shark 7over the 8brightly 9colored 10rainbow

    The subscript numbers represent the associated lock
    The italicized words are the random words given and the bolded words are the words added to make the sentance coherent
  3. Imagine a picture that displays the sentence
  4. Close your eyes and think through the sentence you constructed 3 or 4 times
